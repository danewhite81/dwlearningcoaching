Doctoral Research

Selected papers

Research from a PhD in Leadership Studies, focused on how AI is reshaping leadership, team dynamics, and the coaching craft.

AI-Augmented Emotional Intelligence: A Framework for Scalable Leadership Assessment in Technology-Driven Organizations

Looks at how AI tools can be built into leadership assessment without losing the human judgment that makes those assessments useful, proposing a framework for scaling emotionally intelligent evaluation across larger organizations.

The Neuroscience of Change: Emotional Intelligence as a Mediator of AI Adoption in Corporate Learning Environments

A mixed-methods study on why people resist organizational change, using neuroscience and emotional intelligence research to explain that resistance and offering implications for how coaches and leaders can support adoption of new technology.

Human-Centered AI in Corporate Training: Designing Emotionally Intelligent Systems That Drive Efficacy Without Eroding Trust

Examines how AI-enabled systems can stay effective and efficient without making employees feel surveilled or replaced, and what that means for the future role of the leadership development practitioner.

Generative AI as Co-Facilitator: Reimagining Leadership Development Through Human-Machine Collaboration

Explores what happens to facilitation and coaching practice when generative AI sits alongside the human facilitator, and how that changes both the design of leadership programs and the skills practitioners need going forward.

Measuring What Matters: Predictive Analytics and Sentiment Modeling for Emotionally Intelligent Leadership Development Programs

Proposes a framework for using predictive analytics and sentiment data to measure whether leadership development programs are actually working, connecting coaching outcomes more directly to business results.
